# Design Brainstorm — Luis Coimbra Professional Website

## Context
Professional website for Luis Coimbra — a strategic advisor, keynote speaker, and technology executive with 30+ years of experience. The site must convey authority, sophistication, and approachability. All content in English. Key sections: Hero, About/CV, Speaking Engagements, Services, Testimonials, and Booking CTA.

## Idea 1: "Editorial Monograph" — Swiss Typographic Modernism

**Design Movement**: Swiss International Typographic Style meets editorial magazine layout.

**Core Principles**:
1. Typographic hierarchy as the primary visual element — large, bold display type creates drama
2. Strict grid system with intentional rule-breaking for emphasis
3. Monochromatic palette with a single accent color for strategic emphasis
4. Content-first: photography and text work in concert, never competing

**Color Philosophy**: Deep charcoal (#1a1a2e) as primary background, warm off-white (#f5f0eb) for text areas, and a refined copper/bronze (#c17f59) as the sole accent — conveying warmth, maturity, and gravitas without corporate coldness.

**Layout Paradigm**: Magazine editorial spread — asymmetric two-column layouts where text and imagery occupy distinct zones. Full-bleed photography sections alternate with tight typographic blocks. Horizontal rules and generous margins create reading rhythm.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Oversized pull-quotes set in a serif display font that span the full viewport width
2. Horizontal scroll galleries for speaking photos with parallax depth

**Interaction Philosophy**: Measured, deliberate transitions — content reveals through smooth vertical scrolling with staggered fade-ins. No flashy effects; the sophistication is in the timing and easing.

**Animation**: Slow, confident entrance animations (800ms+). Text elements slide in from the left, images fade with a subtle scale. Scroll-triggered reveals with intersection observer. Navigation transitions use cross-fade.

**Typography System**: Display — "Playfair Display" (serif, bold) for headlines and pull-quotes. Body — "Source Sans 3" (sans-serif) for readable paragraphs. Monospace accent — "JetBrains Mono" for stats/numbers.

## Idea 2: "Architectural Blueprint" — Deconstructivist Minimalism

**Design Movement**: Deconstructivist architecture meets digital design — inspired by Zaha Hadid and Daniel Libeskind's angular forms.

**Core Principles**:
1. Angular geometry and diagonal lines create dynamic tension and forward momentum
2. Negative space is used architecturally — it shapes the content rather than just surrounding it
3. Dark mode as default — the content emerges from darkness like stage lighting
4. Layered depth through overlapping elements and z-index play

**Color Philosophy**: Near-black (#0c0f14) base with steel blue (#2d4a7a) mid-tones and electric teal (#00d4aa) as the accent — representing technology, precision, and innovation. White (#ffffff) text cuts through the darkness with authority.

**Layout Paradigm**: Diagonal section dividers and angled content blocks. Hero section uses a dramatic diagonal split — photo on one side, text on the other, divided by an angular line. Sections overlap slightly, creating depth. Content containers have subtle rotation (1-2 degrees) that straightens on hover.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Geometric clip-path shapes on images and section dividers
2. Thin luminous border lines that trace content boundaries like architectural blueprints
3. Stats/metrics displayed in large monospace type with animated counting

**Interaction Philosophy**: Precision-driven — elements respond to scroll position with mathematical accuracy. Hover states reveal hidden layers. Navigation feels like moving through architectural spaces.

**Animation**: Sharp, geometric transitions — elements slide in along diagonal axes. Numbers count up when scrolled into view. Section transitions use clip-path reveals. Subtle parallax on background elements creates spatial depth.

**Typography System**: Display — "Space Grotesk" (geometric sans) for bold headlines. Body — "DM Sans" for clean readability. Accent — "Space Mono" for technical details and metrics.

## Idea 3: "Warm Authority" — Contemporary Executive Presence

**Design Movement**: Contemporary corporate luxury — inspired by high-end consulting firms and executive personal brands like McKinsey partners' profiles.

**Core Principles**:
1. Warmth through materiality — subtle textures, soft shadows, and natural tones create approachability
2. Generous whitespace communicates confidence and clarity of thought
3. Photography as storytelling — large, immersive images of speaking engagements anchor each section
4. Progressive disclosure — content unfolds naturally as the user scrolls, building narrative

**Color Philosophy**: Warm ivory (#faf8f5) as the primary canvas, deep navy (#0f1b2d) for text and headers, and a rich amber (#d4a853) for CTAs and highlights — the combination of navy and amber evokes trust, wisdom, and prestige. Soft warm grays (#e8e4df) for subtle section backgrounds.

**Layout Paradigm**: Full-width immersive sections that alternate between image-heavy and text-focused blocks. The hero is a dramatic full-viewport image with overlaid text. Content sections use a wide single-column with generous margins. Cards and service blocks use a staggered masonry-like arrangement rather than rigid grids.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Frosted glass navigation bar that blurs the content beneath
2. Subtle grain texture overlay on solid-color sections for tactile warmth
3. Gold/amber underline animations on headings that draw the eye

**Interaction Philosophy**: Organic and flowing — scroll animations feel natural, like turning pages of a premium book. Hover effects are gentle lifts and glows. The booking CTA pulses subtly, drawing attention without aggression.

**Animation**: Smooth, organic entrance animations — elements rise gently (translateY) with opacity transitions. Staggered children animations create a cascading reveal. Images have a subtle zoom effect on scroll. The navigation smoothly transitions from transparent to frosted glass.

**Typography System**: Display — "Cormorant Garamond" (elegant serif) for headlines and section titles. Body — "Outfit" (modern geometric sans) for paragraphs and UI elements. The contrast between ornate serif and clean sans creates a bridge between tradition and modernity.

## Selected Approach: Idea 3 — "Warm Authority"

This approach best represents Luis Coimbra's brand: an experienced executive who combines strategic depth with human warmth. The navy/amber palette conveys trust and prestige, the immersive photography showcases his speaking presence, and the elegant typography bridges tradition with modernity — perfectly reflecting his ITA engineering background and forward-thinking advisory work.
